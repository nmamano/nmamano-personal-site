About me

I am a computer scientist specialized in algorithm design. I received a PhD in computer science from UCI, as part of the Theory group. I was co-advised by Professors David Eppstein and Michael Goodrich. My research dealt with graph algorithms and computational geometry.

Before that, I got a bachelor's degree in CS from UPC in my hometown of Barcelona. I conducted my senior thesis as a visitor at UCI under Professor Wayne Hayes.

Research interests

My dissertation, “New Applications of the Nearest-neighbor Chain Algorithm” studies how we can "relax" the selection rule of a greedy algorithm and still obtain the same solution. We pair this with an algorithmic technique, nearest-neighbor chains, based on which we give new alternative algorithms to greedy algorithms for many classic problems.
